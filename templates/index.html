Welcome to the NBA Awards Predictor!

This webpage was created in an attempt to utlize Machine Learning for the National Basketball Association.

By analyzing data beginning from the 1980-81 season up to the current season (2019-2020), we have trained models to predict which players will be candidates for Most Valuable Player and Rookie of the Year in the upcoming season. We created models utilizing box score stats (otherwise regarded to as "Base Stats") and advanced stats (such as Win Shares & VORP, among others).

The world of basketball is continually evolving with the integration of science for the optimization of its athletes, especially when concerned with their health and their ability to maintain their longevity. It is now commonplace for star athletes to be rested for "meaningless" games in order to survive the grueling marathon that is the NBA Regular Season. Although controversial, the decision to rest star players has been influenced by Data Analytics. Elite level teams such as the 2015-2016 Golden State Warriors began tracking their players’ practice performance throughout the season. Questionnaires were given to the players by the Director of Athletic Performance Keke Lyles with the purpose of tracking their fatigue levels. Additionally, they had been collecting frames from NBA Broadcast cameras and wearable technology with the purpose of analyzing biomechanical movements from the players. Wearable technology provided by Catapult Sports had a microprocessor capable of collecting and parsing more than 1,000 data points per second. At the time of this CBS Sports Article written by Ken Berger, half of the league’s teams (30 teams total) had implemented the tracking devices to determine fatigue levels of their players.

By publishing years worth of box score data, Basketball Reference has become a great source for this project. By aggregating and cleaning the data, we were able to take a closer look at what statistical categories have the most influence in determining a player's effectiveness throughout the season.

Advanced Statistics

Stat Abbreviation Definition
PER Player Efficiency Rating. Developed by John Hollinger, is a per-minute rating that sums up all a player's positive accomplishments and subtracts the negative accomplishments
TS% True Shooting Percentage. True Shooting Percentage is a measure of shooting efficiency that takes into account field goals, 3-point field goals, and free throws.
3PAr 3 Point Attempt Rate. The percentage of a player’s total Field Goal Attempts that were 3 Point Attempts
FTr Free Throw Rate. A ratio of a player’s Free Throw Attempts to Field Goal Attempts.
ORB% Offensive Rebound Percentage. Offensive rebound percentage is an estimate of the percentage of available offensive rebounds a player grabbed while he was on the floor.
DRB% Defensive Rebound Percentage. Defensive rebound percentage is an estimate of the percentage of available defensive rebounds a player grabbed while he was on the floor.
TRB% Total Rebound Percentage. Total rebound percentage is an estimate of the percentage of available rebounds a player grabbed while he was on the floor.
AST% Assist Percentage. Assist percentage is an estimate of the percentage of teammate field goals a player assisted while he was on the floor.
STL% Steal Percentage. Steal Percentage is an estimate of the percentage of opponent possessions that end with a steal by the player while he was on the floor.
BLK % Block Percentage. Block percentage is an estimate of the percentage of opponent two-point field goal attempts blocked by the player while he was on the floor.
TOV% Turnover Percentage. Turnover percentage is an estimate of turnovers per 100 plays
USG% Player Usage Percentage. Usage Percentage is an estimate of the percentage of team plays used by a player while he was on the floor.
WS Win Shares. Developed by Dean Oliver, it is a measure of a player's contribution to their teams wins. A team's total wins is roughly the sum of all of its players' win shares.
OWS Offensive Win Shares. An advanced statistic developed by Dean Oliver
DWS Defensive Win Shares. Defensive Win Shares to players is based on Dean Oliver's Defensive Rating. Defensive Rating is an estimate of the player's points allowed per 100 defensive possessions
WS/48 Win Shares per 48 Minutes (1 Full Basketball Game)
BPM Box Score Plus or Minus. Estimated Player Contribution while on the Floor. Variables are weighted differently depending on player's position.
OBPM Offensive Box Score Plus or Minus. Similar to BPM, it uses the same variables but with weighed differently
DBPM Defensive Box Score Plus or Minus. BPM - OBPM
VORP Value Over Replacement Player is an estimate of each player's overall contribution to the team, measured vs. what a theoretical "replacement player" would provide
